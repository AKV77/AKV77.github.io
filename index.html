Bio

About Me

Hello, I am Ajay Kumar Verma, a PhD researcher in Civil & Environmental Engineering at Carleton University. My work lies at the intersection of computational mathematics, high-performance computing, and Bayesian inference for complex dynamical systems.

I develop scalable computational frameworks to address challenges in public health and engineering. My research focuses on Bayesian parameter estimation, inverse problems, uncertainty quantification, and large-scale simulations using MPI-based and finite element frameworks such as PETSc, FEniCSx, and FreeFEM.

I am particularly interested in non-local PDE formulations, kernel-based dispersal models, and multiscale approaches that couple spatial dynamics with network-level mobility. My current work applies these methods to geospatial epidemic modeling, including coupled SEIRD systems calibrated to real-world COVID-19 data.
